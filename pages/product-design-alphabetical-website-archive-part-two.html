Product design alphabetical websites archive [Part 2]

4 October 2021

As a product designer, I have a wide range of bookmarked websites to inspire, motivate and learn. The other day, I took a look at them and wonder if I could create an article about these stunning sites. So...

...Continue with my beloved and inspiring websites archive. Part 2 has come to town.

Below are the last 13 top-tier alphabetical websites (from N to Z) I use to boost my skills and widen my view.

N. Notion

Notion is an all-in-one workspace that helps people to work the way they want to. It is known for its flexibility, clean design, and personalization. In a world where collaboration is key, meet Notion.

O. Onteractive

Onteractive is a Vietnamese creative agency aiming to deliver high-quality and outstanding works. Their highlighted product consists of product design, visual identity, and 3D production.

P. Phosphor Icons

Phosphor is a free icon collection that stands out for the consistency of its icons in terms of style and scale. Phosphor's website is also interestingly interactive and supportive.

Q. Quillbot

Quillbot is a free writing assistant that keeps my writing clean and painless. Its mission is to enable users to focus on what to write, not how should they write it.

R. removebg

Removing backgrounds is super easy with removebg. Its AI tool simplifies and accelerates workflows, fosters creativity, and enables users to create new elegant products.

S. Slack

Slack is the collaboration hub that brings the right people, information, and tools together to get work done. Using Slack pushes me to be more productive, streamlines my work, and keeps me updated with my team.

T. Typeeverything

Typeverything is a type foundry focusing on display fonts. Its website is simple, straightforward, and inspiring. Viewing their products and display pulls me back to work to create more beautiful products.

U. Uxlagi

Uxlagi is a website of a Vietnamese senior UX engineer at Google - Viet Anh. The website is his over-10-year experience as a UX engineer. His view on UX is really valuable for newbies by offering viewers with an experienced view on UX and psychology. Appreciate Viet Anh on his contribution to the UX field in general and the website in particular.

V. Venture Harbour

Venture Harbour is a team of marketing, product and engineer experts in England. Their product across fields is amazing and motivational. Their marketing case studies are also insightful and of course, their website is astonishing.

W. Wix

Wix is a cloud-based web development service. Wix allows users to create website and mobile apps by using the drag and drop tools. Users can also add social plug-ins, e-commerce, online marketing, contact forms, e-mail marketing, and community forums to their web sites using a variety of Wix-developed and third-party applications.

X. xFrame

xFrame is a website service providing free Asian contents of stock photography. They contribute to the growth of Asian creativity and help creating a more diverse creative world.

Y. YouWorkForThem

Youworkforthem is source for design resources. I was first introduced this site by my typography instructor for inspiration of font using. Since then, I have been surfing the website to update new design trends, and get inspired.

Z. Zoom

As the coronavirus pandemic has forced millions around the world to stay in their homes, Zoom has reenforced the term videoconferencing to everyone. Zoom provides individuals and groups with teleconferencing, telecommuting, distance education, and social relations.
