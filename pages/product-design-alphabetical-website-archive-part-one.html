Product design alphabetical websites archive [Part 1]

29 September 2021

As a product designer, I have a wide range of bookmarked websites to inspire, motivate and learn. The other day, I took a look at them and wonder if I could create an article about these stunning sites. So...

...Here are the first 13 top-tier alphabetical websites (from "A" to "M") I use to boost my skills and widen my view.

A. awwwards

awwwards is a space where professional teams and individuals submit their innovative web design to be promoted and recognized by the community. This is a great site to seek inspiration and update new trends from the very creative people here.

B. Behance

Behance is the first social media platform for creative work. Designers of different levels, from newbies to professionals, can build portfolios, connect with others, and promote themselves in the community.

C. Coolors

Coolors as their introduction is the super fast color schemes generator. Their random color generator allows creatives to create as well as explore new and trending color palettes.

D. Dribbble

Dribbble is a social networking platform for digital designers and creatives. Unlike Behance, Dribbble focuses on short polished showcases called shots. Shots on Dribbble are instant, short, straightforward, and ideal for designers to share, evolve, and get inspired.

E. Envato Elements

Envato Elements is a perfect store for designers that includes amazing graphic assets like stock videos, graphics, photos, fonts, and loads more elements to support creatives on their design projects.

F. Google Fonts

Google Fonts is a free and open-source font catalog where users can explore, test, and use fonts in more than 135 languages. It showcases individual type designers and foundries. There is also a free and public icons library based on the core Material Design principles and metrics.

G. growth.design

Case studies on growth.design are about the user experience that helps designers, product leaders & marketers to build way more satisfying experiences for customers. The site name is the combination of Growth (a scientific method to improve business metrics) and Design (a human-centered process used to solve problems). This site deserves more attention from the design & marketing community.

H. High Resolution

High Resolution is a limited video series on product design and design thinking. Designers can learn valuable things from the best leader of the most successful companies. This website contains hidden gems for designers to explore.

I. icons - Google Fonts

Material Icons as mentioned before is a very popular icons library. Their icons are designed to be simple, modern, friendly. This library is built for designers & developers to use for a clear & beautiful UI pattern.

J. Josh Glucas

Josh Glucas is a designer at Google. His ability to use copy and graphics is professional and pixel-perfect. Every successful senior designer like Glucas is an ideal model for freshers to devote themselves to their UX career.

K. Kickstarter

Kickstarter is where I find creators' projects & ideas brought to life. What is more inspiring from a list of real-life projects across various fields? For start-ups, this is the ideal marketplace; and for creatives, this is a great mood board.

L. LinkedIn

LinkedIn is a professional social media platform where users can find jobs and business opportunities. Members on LinkedIn can expand their connections, which can lead to long-term professional relationships.

M. Material Design

Material Design is an adaptable system of guidelines, components, and tools that support the best practices of UI design. This system streamlines collaboration between design and development departments to bring appealing products to life.
